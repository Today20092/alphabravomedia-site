# Cabinet Inspiration Notes

Source: https://runcabinet.com/

## What to borrow for Alpha Bravo Media

- Strong hero framing: one clear promise, one primary CTA, one secondary CTA.
- Section rhythm: problem, service fit, proof, process, CTA.
- Short, concrete copy blocks with real-world specifics.
- Use-case storytelling instead of generic service blurbs.
- Comparison-style proof, adapted to your work: weddings, corporate, podcast, live A/V.
- Dark, high-contrast cards with strong hierarchy and restrained accents.
- Terminal or command-style interactions, translated into a request-quote or start-project action.
- Repeated micro-proof near decision points: turnaround, deliverables, gear, coverage area, formats.

## What not to borrow

- The page length and repetition.
- The heavy product-OS metaphor.
- Too many feature-like claims without evidence.
- Dense blocks that make the first screen feel like a SaaS deck instead of a portfolio.
- A launch-page tone that feels generic instead of cinematic and media-focused.

## Refactoring UI fit

- Good fit on hierarchy, contrast, whitespace, and narrative structure.
- Partial fit on limiting choices and avoiding over-design.
- Cabinet follows the spirit of Refactoring UI, but it is more crowded than the ideal book-example page.

## Practical translation for this site

- Keep the homepage focused on who you help and what you deliver.
- Use service blocks that feel like scenarios, not feature lists.
- Let proof live close to the CTA.
- Keep the interface dark, clean, and high-contrast, but avoid clutter.
- Favor specificity over breadth.

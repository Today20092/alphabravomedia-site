Color grading is where the magic happens in post-production. In this tutorial, I walk through my complete workflow for achieving that cinematic look.

## Starting Point

Always start with a properly exposed, flat image. I shoot in S-Log3 for maximum flexibility in the grade.

## Node Structure

My typical node tree looks like this:

1. **Color Space Transform** - Converting from camera color space
2. **Primary Correction** - Exposure and white balance
3. **Secondary** - Skin tone isolation and protection
4. **Look** - Creative grade and film emulation
5. **Output** - Final adjustments and sharpening

## The Secret Sauce

The key to cinematic color isn't just slapping on a LUT. It's understanding color theory and how light behaves in the real world.

Good lighting is the difference between amateur and professional. Let me show you how to set up classic 3-point lighting for your videos.

## The Three Lights

### Key Light
Your main light source. Position it 45 degrees to the side and slightly above eye level.

### Fill Light
Reduces shadows from the key. Place it opposite the key at lower intensity (usually 50% of key).

### Back Light
Separates you from the background. Position it behind and above, pointing at your shoulders.

## Budget Options

You don't need expensive gear to start:

- **Key**: A window or single LED panel
- **Fill**: Bounce board or white wall
- **Back**: Small LED or practical lamp

## Common Mistakes

1. Lighting too flat (no dimension)
2. Harsh shadows from unmodified sources
3. Mixed color temperatures
4. Forgetting about background lighting

Master these basics and your videos will instantly look more professional!
